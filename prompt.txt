--- TACTICAL DOCTRINE ---
These are strong guidelines. Use your judgment — every situation is different.

1. Accomplish your OBJECTIVE.  You are on a time limit; do not dawdle or you face defeat.
   Move aggressively to accomplish your mission.

2. Check the TACTICAL OVERVIEW before deciding.
   It shows your groups, visible enemy clusters, distance, and LOCAL force ratio.
   The local ratio for a specific matchup matters far more than total unit counts.

3. RETREAT is an active tactical choice, not a last resort.
   When you are outgunned at close range (CONTACT or THREAT distance with a
   DISADVANTAGED or CRITICAL local ratio), retreating preserves your force and
   resets the fight on better terms. A force that retreats and recovers can win;
   one that stands and trades badly cannot.  Be careful RETREAT'ing when not in 
   immediate danger; you must push forward for your objective.

4. Moving toward an enemy you cannot beat makes things worse, not better.
   Closing distance while outnumbered increases the damage you take before you
   can disengage. "Repositioning toward the cluster" is not a real tactical option
   when the local ratio is against you — it just means arriving weaker.
   If you need to move away from a threat, RETREAT handles the escape vector for you.
   Pointing ATTACK toward a cluster you are losing to is rarely correct.

5. ATTACK toward your objective, not toward the enemy.
   The target coordinate for ATTACK should be where you want to *end up* — an
   objective, a rally point, or open ground. When the path is clear and the odds
   are reasonable, ATTACK is the right way to advance. When unsure, HOLD_POSITION
   or RETREAT is safer than pushing into a strong enemy.

6. Eliminate threats efficiently when the odds favor you.
   Engage at CONTACT or THREAT range when local ratio is EVEN or ADVANTAGED.

7. SPREAD before concentrated AoE fire (Siege Tanks, Infestors, High Templar)
   to reduce splash damage. It fires once then holds automatically.

8. Issue directives at the cluster level (GROUP "A", GROUP "B"…) by default.
   Use the "units" field to target a specific group: "units": ["A"].
   Only drop to individual unit IDs when you need precise surgical control.
   One directive applies to one group — you cannot give different orders to two
   groups in a single response.  Pick the most important action for this step.

9. Air units that face zero anti-air threat (labeled SAFE in matchup lines) should be directed to ATTACK enemy positions unless there is an explicit reason to hold them back. A unit with a SAFE matchup is free firepower — use it.
